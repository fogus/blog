Ranier Joswig has linked a number of papers suggested by or written by Richard P. Gabriel in the Lisp Sub-Reddit. Although I’ve read a few of those linked, I thought placing the recommendations here would be interesting to my readers, and serve as a personal reminder to study them for research purposes.

  • Howard I. Cannon, Flavors, A non-hierarchical approach to object-oriented programming (PDF)

  • Linda DeMichiel & Richard P. Gabriel, The Common Lisp Object System: An Overview (PDF)

  • Richard P. Gabriel, The Structure of a Programming Language Revolution (PDF)

  • Richard P. Gabriel, Jon L. White, and Daniel G. Bobrow, CLOS: Integrating Object-Oriented and Functional Programming (PDF)

  • Warren Teitelman, PILOT, A Step Toward Man-Computer Symbiosis

I will add more if Mr. Joswig does the same.

Enjoy.
